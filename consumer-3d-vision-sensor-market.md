<p><p>Consumer 3D vision sensors are used in various industries and applications. There are several types of 3D vision sensor technologies available in the market. </p><p>Structured Light uses a projected pattern to capture the depth information of objects. It is commonly used in facial recognition, augmented reality, and object scanning.</p><p>iToF (Indirect Time-of-Flight) measures the time it takes for light to bounce off objects and return to the sensor. It is utilized in smartphones for portrait mode photography and gesture recognition.</p><p>dToF (Direct Time-of-Flight) calculates depth by measuring the flight time of light directly. It finds applications in autonomous vehicles, object detection, and virtual reality.</p><p>Other types of 3D vision sensors include stereo vision, laser triangulation, and photogrammetry, among others, which are used for various specific purposes.</p></p>
